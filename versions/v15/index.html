EasyDo 365 — v15 (Mainframe)

An IBM punch card meets a minimal techie terminal. Manila card stock (#F2EAD3) for hero surfaces with printed-ink navy text and dashed-rule perforations. CRT amber `#FF6B00` as the "punched / active" accent. IBM Plex Mono for every number, time, code, and label. Pure white canvas underneath — the techie counterpoint.

The punch hero looks like a real 80-column attendance card: column markers, dashed perforations, a serial-number header (EASYDO-365 / ATTENDANCE / SERIAL 0001), and the "hole" — a deep-recessed circular punch through the manila that lights amber when active. Sharp 0–4px corners, mono everywhere, rectangular not pilled. Buttons cast 4px-offset shadows in the brand color. Status chips are rectangular code labels, not pills.

v15 hero idea

  • Punch hero is a literal IBM punch card — manila + column markers + perforations.
  • CRT amber for the active "hole" / brand color.
  • IBM Plex Mono for every time, number, code, and label.

Retro signals

  • Manila card stock #F2EAD3 with warm tan edges.
  • Dashed rules instead of solid — they feel like perforations.
  • Section heads in [ BRACKET TYPOGRAPHY ], COBOL/IBM-style.
  • 4px-offset hard shadows on buttons + chips — mechanical "stamped" feel.

Techie signals

  • Pure white canvas under everything — never warm.
  • Sharp 0–4px corners. Pill is reserved for round status dots only.
  • Tabular numerals with 'ss01','zero' mono features.
  • Greeting prefix >, comment prefix //.

What still carries

  • 5-tab IA + permission model (v7 lineage, intact).
  • Attendance hairline logbook + announcements screen.
  • Lightning bolt FAB icon (v13 keep) — now amber-on-ink.
